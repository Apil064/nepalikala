This Thangka depicts the first light striking the Himalayan peaks — a moment of profound spiritual significance in Buddhist teaching. Karma Lama spent eight months creating this work, grinding lapis lazuli, malachite, and cinnabar by hand, following the same recipe his master passed down over 30 years of apprenticeship.

The gold detailing — 24-karat gold leaf — was applied using a yak-tail brush in the final weeks, a process requiring a still mind and steady hand.

Karma Lama is a third-generation thangka master based in Boudhanath, Kathmandu. Trained from age 12 under master Tenzin Gyatso, his works are held in private collections in 12 countries and have been exhibited in Kathmandu, London, and New York.
