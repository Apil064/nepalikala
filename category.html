About the Style

Thangka: Sacred Art

Thangka is a Tibetan Buddhist painting on cotton or silk appliqué, usually depicting Buddhist deities, scenes, or mandalas. Dating back over 1,000 years, the tradition requires years of training under a master and uses mineral pigments that have remained unchanged for centuries.

"Every thangka is a map — not of geography, but of the mind seeking liberation."
