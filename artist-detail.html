About Karma Lama

Karma Lama was initiated into the thangka tradition at age 12 under the guidance of master Tenzin Gyatso in Boudhanath — one of the holiest Buddhist sites in the world. Over 18 years, he has mastered the precise geometry of Tibetan iconography, the grinding of lapis lazuli and malachite into pigments, and the meditative state required to render sacred beings with the accuracy the tradition demands.

His work is held in private collections across twelve countries and has been exhibited in Kathmandu, London, and New York. In 2022, the Nepal Academy of Fine Arts awarded him the Kala Prativa Purashkar — one of Nepal's highest artistic honours.

"Every brushstroke is a meditation. The painting teaches me as much as I teach it. You cannot rush a thangka. You can only prepare yourself to receive it."

Each of Karma's originals takes between three months and a year to complete. He works only with natural mineral pigments — lapis lazuli for blue, malachite for green, cinnabar for red — mixed with yak-hide glue using a recipe unchanged for over 500 years.
